Understanding

The Landscape of Depression

Depression manifests differently for everyone. Understanding its forms helps us recognize it in ourselves and others.

Major Depressive Disorder

More than persistent sadness, MDD affects cognition, physical health, and daily functioning. It's characterized by episodes lasting at least two weeks, often recurring throughout life without treatment.

Cognitive fog Anhedonia Sleep disruption

Persistent Depressive Disorder

A chronic form lasting at least two years. Less severe symptoms but longer duration, often feeling like "this is just who I am."

"The grayness becomes your normal."

Seasonal Affective

Depression related to seasonal changes, typically winter. Linked to reduced sunlight affecting circadian rhythms.

Postpartum Depression

Occurs after childbirth, affecting 1 in 7 mothers. Beyond "baby blues," it includes severe mood changes, exhaustion, and anxiety that interfere with daily care.

Pathways Forward

Seek Professional Guidance

Therapy and medication are evidence-based treatments, not signs of weakness.

Maintain Routine Anchors

Simple structures—waking at the same time, showering—create stability when emotions feel chaotic.

Practice Self-Compassion

Speak to yourself as you would a dear friend. You are learning, not failing.

Avoid These Traps

Isolation

Withdrawing intensifies depression. Connection, even when difficult, is medicinal.

Self-Medication

Alcohol and substances temporarily numb but ultimately deepen depressive episodes.

Catastrophizing

"This will never end" is a symptom, not a truth. Thoughts are not facts.
