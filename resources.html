Understanding & Healing

Knowledge is power

Understanding depression is the first step toward healing. Here you'll find evidence-based information, professional resources, and pathways to support.

Understanding

What is Depression?

Depression is a complex medical condition affecting mood, thinking, and physical health. It is not a sign of weakness or a condition one can simply "snap out" of.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness or emptiness lasting weeks
  • Loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities
  • Significant changes in sleep or appetite
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt

Potential Causes

  • Biological: Chemical imbalances in the brain
  • Genetic: Family history of depression
  • Environmental: Trauma, stress, or major life changes
  • Medical: Chronic illness, certain medications
  • Hormonal: Postpartum, thyroid issues, menopause

Types of Depression

Recovery

Evidence-Based Treatments

Psychotherapy (Talk Therapy)

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Interpersonal Therapy (IPT), and Psychodynamic Therapy help identify thought patterns and develop coping strategies.

Medication

Antidepressants can help correct chemical imbalances. SSRIs, SNRIs, and atypical antidepressants require medical supervision and typically take 4-6 weeks to show full effects.

Brain Stimulation Therapies

For treatment-resistant depression, options include Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS), and Vagus Nerve Stimulation.

Lifestyle Interventions

Regular Exercise

30 minutes of moderate exercise three times weekly can be as effective as medication for mild depression.

Sleep Hygiene

Consistent sleep schedules, limiting screen time before bed, and creating a restful environment support mood regulation.

Social Connection

Isolation worsens depression. Even brief interactions with friends, family, or support groups provide protective benefits.

Light Exposure

Morning sunlight helps regulate circadian rhythms and boost serotonin production.
